[Andrew@sysfuck ~]$ First and foremost – why did I decide to use Linux?

It’s quite simple. After getting a desktop and running Windows for maybe two weeks, I was already done with it.
No exaggeration. The whole experience felt like I was a slave to my own machine. No control. Ads. Telemetry. MS Recall.
Nah just fuck off.

I wanted control.

[Andrew@sysfuck ~]$ The Hard Switch: Arch + KDE

So I flushed Windows down the toilet and installed Arch. Straight to it.
KDE Plasma was my first environment. Looked good, worked well.

However people say Arch is hard. Not really. If you can read the Arch Wiki and you’re not scared of a terminal,
you’ll be fine. KDE made it easy too – GUIs for most settings.

[Andrew@sysfuck ~]$ But KDE wasn't it...

KDE was powerful, yeah – but bloated. Too many things I never used.
Clipboard history, file indexers, fancy effects... stuff I didn’t need or want.

I don’t like bloat. I like minimalism. I like understanding what’s running and why.

[Andrew@sysfuck ~]$ The Move to DWM

In May 2025, I switched to DWM. The dynamic window manager from suckless.

And I love it.

No crap. Just windows and control.
I wrote my own statusbar scripts, finally achieving the feeling of actually owning my Computer.

Final Thoughts

"Linux isn’t just an OS. It’s a mindset."
- Sun Zhu

"A computer is like an air-conditioning - it becomes useless when you open windows"
- Linus Torvalds

On Windows, you're stuck in someone else's idea of a computer.
On Linux, it's yours.

[Andrew@sysfuck ~]$ TL;DR
Never going back.

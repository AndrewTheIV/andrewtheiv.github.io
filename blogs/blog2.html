[Andrew@sysfuck ~]$ Why I Hate Bloat

Let’s start with a simple question:
What even is bloat?

Bloat is the opposite of minimalism. It fills your system with rarely used, unnecessary features. Stuff you never asked for, sitting — eating up resources, draining memory, wasting your time.

But what about a real-world example? A calculator app.

Let’s be honest — how often do you actually use the preinstalled calculator on your OS? Maybe once in a week, month or less? Most of the time, when I need to do a quick calculation, I just type it into a search engine. Done. Fast. No need for some bloated UI with history, themes, or currency converters!!!

Easy to sum up: If I don’t use it, and it just sits there — that’s bloat.

Now, let’s scale this "problem" up. A lot of operating systems such as Windows come preloaded with all kinds of shit:

  • Xbox Game Bar
  • Cortana
  • OneDrive
  • Widgets

All these services, constantly running in the background, sending data and using power — for what? My god...I didn’t ask for any of this.

And it’s not just Microsoft. KDE Plasma — as beautiful and feature-rich as it is — comes with clipboard managers, file indexers, notification daemons, and a whole pile of background services that I DONT need.

That’s what led me to switch from KDE Plasma to DWM. Suckless Dynamic Window Manager.

If you agree with my view, I’d highly recommend checking out
suckless.org — the holy grail of programs...

Final Thoughts

Bloat kills performance, eats freedom, and fucks your system down.
Minimalism is freedom!!!!
I do not need all of these crappy features.
You need less crap.
